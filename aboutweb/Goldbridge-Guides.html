Our Methodology
How we research and compare tools
Every Gold Bridge resource follows a five-step editorial process designed to be transparent and useful — not promotional.
01

Define the use case

Start with a specific user need — team size, budget range, workflow — and scope the comparison to that context.

02

Compare pricing & features

Break down plans, limits, and what each tier actually includes. Note hidden costs and upgrade triggers.

03

Assess fit and limitations

Evaluate who should use each tool — and who should not. No tool is right for everyone, and we say so.

04

Document criteria

Publish the criteria used, include update dates, and explain how editorial conclusions were reached.

05

Disclose all relationships

Affiliate and commercial relationships are disclosed alongside the content they appear in. Always.

Project management checklist for 5-person teams: format preview

This page shows the checklist structure for our upcoming project management evaluation resource. The checklist is in development and is not yet a complete published resource.

Who this checklist is for

Small teams of around 5 people evaluating project management tools for the first time, or switching from informal tracking (spreadsheets, email threads, sticky notes) to a dedicated tool. This checklist helps structure the decision without overwhelming small teams with enterprise criteria.

Checklist areas

The published checklist will cover the following evaluation areas. Each area will include specific questions to ask during a trial or demo:

Task visibility

Can every team member see what needs to be done without asking someone?

Ownership and deadlines

Can tasks be clearly assigned with due dates that trigger reminders?

Client access

Can you share project visibility with a client without giving internal access?

File sharing

Can documents be attached to tasks or does your team need a separate file system?

Notifications

Are notifications configurable, or do they overwhelm small teams by default?

Recurring tasks

Does the tool handle repeating work without manual re-creation each week?

Time tracking

Is time tracking built in, or does your team need a separate tool?

Reporting

Can you generate a progress summary for stakeholders without exporting to a spreadsheet?

Integrations

Does the tool connect to your existing email, calendar and communication tools?

Adoption difficulty

How long until your team actually uses it daily instead of reverting to old habits?

Red flags when choosing a PM tool

  • Requires a minimum number of paid seats larger than your team
  • Core features locked behind enterprise pricing tiers
  • No free trial or demo environment
  • Complex setup that requires dedicated admin time
  • No way to export your data if you want to switch later

Pricing questions to ask

  • What is the per-user monthly cost on an annual plan?
  • What features are excluded from the tier you are evaluating?
  • Are guest or client seats free or paid?
  • What happens to your data if you cancel?

This checklist structure is in progress. Target publication: Q3 2026. The checklist areas above represent planned content, not yet a finalized evaluation.

Scheduling software comparison scorecard: format preview

This page shows the scorecard structure for our upcoming scheduling software comparison. The scorecard is in the research phase and is not yet a complete published resource.

Who this scorecard is for

Consultants, service businesses, coaches and freelancers who book client appointments and need a reliable scheduling tool. This scorecard helps compare the options that matter most for one-on-one and small-team booking workflows.

What the scorecard evaluates

Each scheduling tool will be scored across these criteria. The scoring methodology will be documented in the published version:

Scoring criteriaWhat we evaluate
Booking page flexibilityCustomization, branding, multiple event types
Calendar integrationsGoogle Calendar, Outlook, iCal sync reliability
Automated remindersEmail and SMS confirmations, follow-up sequences
Team schedulingRound-robin, collective availability, team pages
Payment supportStripe/PayPal integration, deposits, prepayment
Cancellation / reschedulingBuffer times, policies, client self-service
Pricing transparencyClear plan comparison, no hidden upgrade triggers
Client experienceBooking flow simplicity, mobile friendliness
IntegrationsCRM, video conferencing, forms, payment processors
Setup timeTime from signup to functional booking page

What buyers should check before choosing

  • Does the free tier include the calendar integration you use?
  • Are reminder emails customizable or fixed templates?
  • Can you collect payment at the time of booking?
  • What happens if you exceed the plan's appointment limit?
  • Can clients reschedule without contacting you directly?
